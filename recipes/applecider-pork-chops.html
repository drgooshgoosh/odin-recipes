Apple Cider Pork Chops

Description

These pork chops are seared and then glazed with a tangy-sweet apple cider reduction. The glaze, combined with Dijon mustard and fresh thyme, gives the pork a flavorful and juicy finish, making it a delicious autumn meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in pork chops
  • 1 1/2 cups apple cider
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Season pork chops with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat and sear the chops for 3-4 minutes per side.
  2. Remove the pork chops from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add garlic and thyme, and saut√© for 1 minute.
  4. Pour in apple cider and bring it to a simmer, stirring in Dijon mustard.
  5. Reduce the sauce until it thickens, then add butter to enrich the glaze.
  6. Return the pork chops to the skillet, coat them in the glaze, and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Serve immediately, spooning extra glaze over the chops.
